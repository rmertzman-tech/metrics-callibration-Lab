Welcome to the Metrics Calibration Lab

Before you use any of the case simulation apps, this lab builds your intuition for what the simulation metrics actually mean — not as definitions to memorize, but as numbers you can feel in terms of real human situations.

Seven rounds. Each one gives you a situation described in plain language, asks you to predict the numbers before seeing the results, runs the actual simulation, and then explains the gap between your prediction and what the structure produced.

By the end, when the Hanna simulation shows λ Raw = 0.23, you will already know — from your own calibration experience — exactly what that number means in human terms.

The seven metrics you will calibrate:

λ Raw — Coordination Capacity Available
λ Raw
The Situation
The Overruled Nurses. A hospital unit has 14 experienced nurses. Over the past eight months, a new administrator — with no clinical background — has required that every clinical recommendation be approved before implementation. In practice, 80% of recommendations are delayed or overruled, regardless of patient outcome. The nurses still have the same knowledge and skills they always had. But their ability to act on that knowledge has been structurally blocked.

Before we show you any numbers: how much coordination capacity do you think exists in this system right now?
What λ Raw Measures
λ Raw is the coordination capacity available in a system — how much the structural conditions allow agents to act effectively together, based on entropy (how chaotic/unpredictable the environment is), stress loading, and which behaviors the institution rewards or suppresses. It is not about individual skill or willingness. It is about what the structure makes possible. Range: 0.0 (no capacity available) to 1.0 (full capacity).

λ Perceived — Coordination Capacity Reaching Decisions
λ Perceived
The Situation
The Hidden Safety Reports. An engineering company has highly skilled safety analysts. They write detailed reports identifying structural risks in the company's products. The reports are complete, accurate, and based on solid data. But they pass through three layers of middle management before reaching the executives who make decisions — and at each layer, the most alarming findings are softened to avoid "unnecessary alarm." By the time the reports reach decision-makers, the critical warnings have been removed.

Notice: the analysts have real capability. The capability exists. The question is: how much of it is actually reaching the decisions that matter?
What λ Perceived Measures — and Why It Differs from λ Raw
λ Perceived measures how much of the available coordination capacity is actually reaching decisions. In a healthy system, λ Raw and λ Perceived are close together. When institutions filter, soften, or block information before it reaches decision-makers, a gap opens. The gap (λ Raw − λ Perceived) is the suppression gap — structurally measurable knowledge being prevented from influencing outcomes. In the engineers' case: λ Raw may be moderate (they have real capability) but λ Perceived will be much lower (the capability is filtered before it reaches decisions).

SMALL GAP
Gap 0.05–0.15
Some filtering but most capability is reaching decisions.

Healthy system. Capability and perceived contribution closely aligned.

Suppression Gap — What Doesn't Get Heard
Suppression Gap
The Situation
The Junior Faculty. A university department has 12 faculty members. The five most junior members hold the most current expertise in the field — they completed their degrees recently and have skills the senior faculty do not have. But departmental culture requires deference to seniority. In every committee meeting, junior faculty self-censor before speaking: they gauge what senior faculty want to hear and adjust accordingly. Their knowledge exists. It is just rarely expressed in forms that could change decisions.

This situation has been going on for four years. How much of the department's available expertise do you think is actually reaching its decisions?
The Suppression Gap — Why It Matters Ethically
The suppression gap (λ Raw − λ Perceived) is not just a technical measure. It is the structurally measurable Kantian violation in the simulation suite. Kant's Formula of Humanity says: treat every rational agent as an end in themselves, never merely as a means. A suppression gap of 0.40 means that 40% of the available rational agency in this system is being structurally prevented from contributing to decisions — treated, effectively, as if it does not matter. The gap does not require anyone to intend harm. The structure produces it whether or not any individual chose it.

Morphic Suppression — Learned Silence
Morphic Suppression
The Situation
Five Years of Being Ignored. A city planning department employs community liaisons — people hired specifically to bring neighborhood concerns into planning decisions. For five years, their reports have been received, filed, and consistently set aside when decisions are made. Nothing the liaisons report changes any outcome. Initially they wrote thorough, detailed reports. After two years, the reports got shorter. After three years, they stopped including the most controversial findings. After five years, they primarily report what they expect the planners want to hear.

Nobody told them to do this. They learned it. The suppression was structural, and the response to it became automatic.
What Morphic Suppression Measures — and Why It Builds Over Time
Morphic suppression is accumulated learned self-censorship. It builds across simulation steps — each step representing a period of time where the agent's deliberate contribution consistently fails to reach outcomes. The brain's predictive machinery updates: "trying does not produce results." Eventually agents stop expressing capabilities they still have, because the expectation of futility has been incorporated into their predictive model. This is not weakness or laziness. It is the brain doing exactly what it is designed to do — calibrating predictions to match experience. The ethical claim: the institution produced this change without any individual choosing it.

Morphic Fidelity — Identity Signal Surviving
Morphic Fidelity
The Situation — Two Versions
The Isolated Organizer. A community organizer in an authoritarian country is under sustained government surveillance and harassment. Her previous organizational network has been systematically dismantled. She is still committed to her values. She still has her skills. But the community around her — the relational scaffolding that sustained and confirmed her identity — is being severed piece by piece.

Version A: She has no external support — no international network, no allied organizations, no communication with others who share her framework.
Version B: A small international solidarity organization is aware of her case and publicly advocates for her. Her work is documented and known outside the country.

In both versions the suppression is severe. The question is: how much of her original identity signal survives?
What Morphic Fidelity Measures
Morphic fidelity measures how much of the original coordination capacity signal is surviving under suppression — the ratio of what remains coherent to what is being eroded. High fidelity under high suppression means the identity is holding: something is maintaining the coherence of the signal despite the pressure applied to it. Low fidelity means the suppression is winning — the identity is fragmenting. In this lab we compute it as: λ Raw ÷ (λ Raw + morphic suppression) — the proportion of the capacity that is not being lost to accumulated learned silence. Range: 0.0 (identity completely fragmented) to 1.0 (identity fully intact).

OS2 Bridge — Cross-Worldview Convergence
OS2 Bridge + Friction
The Situation
The Neighborhood Coalition. Five groups are working to clean up a polluted creek running through their neighborhood. They have completely different reasons for caring: an evangelical church (stewardship of God's creation), a Somali immigrant community (practical water access for families), a group of environmental science students (ecological restoration for its own sake), a Latino community organization (environmental justice — this neighborhood was deliberately targeted for industrial dumping), and a neighborhood homeowners association (property values).

They disagree about almost everything — religion, politics, what justice means, whose fault this is. But they all show up on Saturday mornings to clean the creek.

What do you predict happens to coordination across these groups when the shared task is present?
OS2 Bridge and Friction — The Key Counterintuitive Relationship
OS2 Bridge measures whether agents with genuinely different frameworks are moving toward the same outcomes — not whether they agree, but whether their different capabilities are converging on shared goals. Friction measures the translation cost of that difference — how much effort it takes for different frameworks to communicate effectively enough to act together. Here is the counterintuitive truth: high friction and high OS2 Bridge can coexist. The coalition experiences real friction — it takes effort to coordinate across five genuinely different worldviews. But the shared task (cleaning the creek) makes that friction cost worth paying. The question is never "is friction high?" but "is the coordination worth the friction cost?"
